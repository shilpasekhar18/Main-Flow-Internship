Pasta al Pomodoro Recipe

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti or your favorite pasta
  • 1kg ripe tomatoes (San Marzano or Roma, ideally)
  • 4 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves (torn or whole)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Parmesan cheese (for serving, optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Tomatoes: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Score a small "X" on the bottom of each tomato with a knife. Place the tomatoes in the boiling water for 30-60 seconds until the skins begin to peel. Remove the tomatoes and immediately place them in an ice water bath to cool. Once cooled, peel off the skins, remove the seeds (if desired), and chop the tomatoes into small pieces or crush them by hand for a more rustic sauce.
  2. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
  3. Prepare the Sauce: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped garlic (and onion if using) and cook for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant and lightly golden, but not burnt. Add the chopped or crushed tomatoes to the skillet. Stir everything together and season with salt and pepper. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly and the flavors meld together.
  4. Add the Basil: Once the sauce has simmered, add the fresh basil leaves and stir them into the sauce. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a bit of the reserved pasta water to thin it out.
  5. Combine the Pasta and Sauce: Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the sauce. Toss everything together until the pasta is well-coated with the tomato sauce. If necessary, add a little more of the reserved pasta water to help the sauce cling to the pasta.
  6. Serve: Transfer the Pasta al Pomodoro to serving plates. Optionally, garnish with extra fresh basil and a drizzle of olive oil. Serve with freshly grated Parmesan cheese if you like.
  7. Optional Variations: Red Pepper Flakes: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes for a hint of heat. Butter: Stir in a tablespoon of butter at the end for added richness. Protein: Add grilled chicken, shrimp, or meatballs if you prefer some protein.
  8. Optional Additions: Beans: Add refried beans or black beans inside the enchiladas for extra filling. Vegetables: Add sautéed veggies like bell peppers or spinach for a veggie-packed version. Spice: Add sliced jalapeños, hot sauce, or spicy salsa for a kick of heat.
  9. Tips: Use the ripest tomatoes you can find for the best flavor, or substitute with high-quality canned tomatoes (San Marzano) if fresh tomatoes are not available. Don't overcook the garlic; it should be fragrant but not browned to avoid bitterness.
