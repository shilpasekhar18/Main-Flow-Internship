Enchiladas Recipe

Ingredients

  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 2 cups cooked shredded chicken (or beef, pork, or cheese for filling)
  • 2 cups enchilada sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (Cheddar or Monterey Jack)
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions (optional)
  • 2 eggs (optional, for serving)
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro (for garnish)
  • 1/4 cup sour cream (optional, for serving)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying tortillas)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the enchilada sauce in a saucepan over medium heat until warm. If you prefer homemade sauce, you can use a mixture of tomatoes, chili powder, garlic, onion, and broth cooked down into a rich sauce.
  2. Heat a bit of vegetable oil in a pan and lightly fry each tortilla for about 15-20 seconds per side. This makes them more pliable and prevents them from getting soggy.
  3. Drain the tortillas on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  4. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Take each fried tortilla and place a spoonful of the shredded chicken (or filling of choice) in the center. Add a bit of chopped onions (optional) to the filling. Roll the tortilla tightly and place it seam-side down in a baking dish.
  5. Once all tortillas are rolled and placed in the baking dish, pour the warm enchilada sauce over the top, making sure to cover the enchiladas completely. Sprinkle shredded cheese evenly over the top.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Remove the enchiladas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with chopped cilantro, sour cream, and any additional toppings like sliced avocado or jalapeños. Serve hot with rice, beans, or a side salad.
  8. Optional Additions: Beans: Add refried beans or black beans inside the enchiladas for extra filling. Vegetables: Add sautéed veggies like bell peppers or spinach for a veggie-packed version. Spice: Add sliced jalapeños, hot sauce, or spicy salsa for a kick of heat.
